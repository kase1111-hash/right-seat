# R002 — Asymmetric Power/Trim Disagreement

## Real-World Failure Mode

In multi-engine operations, asymmetric thrust (one engine producing significantly more power than the other) requires compensating rudder and aileron trim. Failure to set appropriate trim during single-engine operations creates an unsafe flight condition: if the pilot becomes distracted or incapacitated, the aircraft will yaw and roll toward the dead engine. Conversely, significant trim without corresponding power asymmetry may indicate a trim runaway or a forgotten configuration from a previous maneuver.

## How the Rule Works

R002 monitors throttle lever positions, engine RPM, and trim settings across all engines. It detects:

1. **Power asymmetry without trim**: Throttle differential >10% or RPM differential >100 RPM, but rudder/aileron trim is near neutral (within ±5%).
2. **Trim without power asymmetry**: Significant trim deflection (exceeding profile threshold) but throttle and RPM are balanced.
3. **Time-based escalation**: The initial detection generates an ADVISORY. If the condition persists for 60 seconds, it escalates to WARNING.

## Severity Levels

| Severity | Condition |
|----------|-----------|
| ADVISORY | Mismatch initially detected |
| WARNING | Mismatch persists for >=60 seconds |

## Applicability

- Multi-engine aircraft only (profile `engine_count` >= 2)
- Airborne only (not applicable in Ground phase)

## Key Thresholds

- Throttle differential: >10% of lever range
- RPM differential: >100 RPM
- Trim neutral zone: ±5%
- Trim significance: `profile.trim.asymmetric_threshold_pct`
- Escalation timer: 60 seconds

## Required SimVars

- `ThrottleLeverPosition` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngRpm` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngCombustion` (per engine)
- `RudderTrimPct`
- `AileronTrimPct`

## Evaluation Interval

5 seconds

## Limitations

- Cannot distinguish intentional single-engine training from actual engine failure
- Trim thresholds are generic; some aircraft have different trim authority ranges
- Does not account for propeller feathering state
