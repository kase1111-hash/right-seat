# R003 — Engine Temperature Trend

## Real-World Failure Mode

Rising cylinder head temperature (CHT) or exhaust gas temperature (EGT) can indicate cooling system degradation, mixture mismanagement, detonation, or impending engine failure. Exceeding redline temperatures causes metallurgical damage to cylinder heads, valves, and exhaust components. Catching a rising temperature trend early gives the pilot time to adjust mixture, reduce power, or open cowl flaps before reaching destructive temperatures.

## How the Rule Works

R003 monitors both absolute temperature values and rate-of-change trends over a 60-second sliding window:

1. **Rate-of-change detection**: Uses the telemetry buffer's `RateOfChange` to compute CHT/EGT rise rate over 60 seconds.
2. **Absolute threshold checks**: Compares current temperatures against percentage thresholds of the profile's redline values.
3. **Climb phase relaxation**: During CLIMB phase, rate-of-change thresholds are relaxed by 1.5x, since rising temperatures during climb are expected.

## Severity Levels

| Severity | Condition |
|----------|-----------|
| ADVISORY | CHT rising faster than profile advisory rate |
| WARNING | CHT rising faster than profile warning rate, OR temperature >= 90% of redline |
| CRITICAL | Temperature >= 95% of redline |

## Applicability

- All engine types
- Airborne only (not applicable in Ground phase)
- Requires engine combustion active

## Key Thresholds

- Advisory rate: `profile.engine.cht_trend_advisory_f_per_min` (converted to Rankine/sec internally)
- Warning rate: `profile.engine.cht_trend_warning_f_per_min` (converted to Rankine/sec internally)
- Absolute warning: >= 90% of `profile.engine.cht_redline_f` (or `egt_redline_f`)
- Absolute critical: >= 95% of redline
- Trend window: 60 seconds
- Climb relaxation factor: 1.5x

## Required SimVars

- `EngCylinderHeadTemperature` (per engine)
- `EngExhaustGasTemperature` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngCombustion` (per engine)

## Evaluation Interval

5 seconds

## Limitations

- Temperature values from SimConnect may be smoothed or delayed compared to real aircraft gauges
- Rate thresholds assume steady-state; rapid power changes (go-around) will cause expected rapid temperature rises that may trigger false positives
- Not all MSFS aircraft model CHT/EGT accurately
