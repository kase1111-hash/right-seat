# R007 — Electrical Degradation

## Real-World Failure Mode

Electrical system failures in general aviation aircraft can cascade rapidly. A failing alternator causes a slow voltage drop that may go unnoticed until avionics start failing. Low bus voltage degrades GPS, communication radios, and glass cockpit displays. Complete electrical failure at night or in IMC is an emergency. Early detection of voltage trends gives pilots time to shed electrical load and plan a precautionary landing.

## How the Rule Works

R007 monitors main bus and battery bus voltages using three strategies:

1. **Absolute minimum breach**: Bus voltage below the profile's absolute minimum triggers CRITICAL (imminent or complete failure).
2. **Sustained voltage trend**: Over a 120-second window, if main bus voltage shows a sustained decrease (rate > 0.001 V/sec, approximately 0.06 V/min), fires WARNING. This catches alternator degradation before it reaches critical levels.
3. **Below normal range**: Main bus voltage below the normal operating range (but above minimum) fires ADVISORY.

## Severity Levels

| Severity | Condition |
|----------|-----------|
| CRITICAL | Main bus < minimum voltage OR Battery bus < minimum voltage |
| WARNING | Sustained voltage decrease trend over 120 seconds |
| ADVISORY | Main bus below normal range |

## Applicability

- All aircraft types
- All flight phases

## Key Thresholds

- Main bus minimum: `profile.electrical.main_bus_minimum_v`
- Main bus normal range: `profile.electrical.main_bus_normal_v` [low, high]
- Battery bus minimum: `profile.electrical.battery_bus_minimum_v`
- Trend window: 120 seconds
- Minimum trend rate: 0.001 V/sec (~0.06 V/min)

## Required SimVars

- `ElectricalMainBusVoltage`
- `ElectricalBatteryBusVoltage`

## Evaluation Interval

10 seconds

## Limitations

- Voltage readings in MSFS may not reflect real-world transient behavior during high-load operations (gear/flap motors)
- The 120-second trend window means very rapid failures may be caught by absolute threshold before trend detection
- DA62 and other 28V aircraft have different voltage ranges than typical 14V GA aircraft (handled via profile)
