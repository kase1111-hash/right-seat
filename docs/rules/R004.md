# R004 — Oil Pressure Anomaly

## Real-World Failure Mode

Oil pressure is the single most critical engine health indicator in piston and turboprop engines. Loss of oil pressure leads to bearing failure, seizure, and catastrophic engine destruction within minutes. Common causes include oil pump failure, oil leak, plugged oil filter bypass, or loss of oil quantity. A divergence pattern (oil temp rising while oil pressure falls) is a strong indicator of lubrication system degradation.

## How the Rule Works

R004 monitors oil pressure and temperature for each engine using three detection strategies:

1. **Absolute minimum breach**: Oil pressure below the profile's minimum threshold triggers an immediate CRITICAL alert.
2. **Rapid pressure drop**: Rate-of-change over 30 seconds exceeds the profile's drop rate threshold.
3. **Temp/pressure divergence**: Oil temperature rising AND oil pressure falling simultaneously, with both rates exceeding meaningful thresholds.
4. **Ground idle suppression**: Low oil pressure on the ground with RPM < 800 is normal (engine at idle) and suppressed.

## Severity Levels

| Severity | Condition |
|----------|-----------|
| CRITICAL | Oil pressure below absolute minimum |
| WARNING | Rapid pressure drop rate exceeded |
| WARNING | Temperature rising + pressure falling simultaneously |

## Applicability

- All engine types
- All phases (with ground idle suppression)

## Key Thresholds

- Absolute minimum: `profile.engine.oil_pressure_minimum_psi`
- Drop rate warning: `profile.engine.oil_pressure_drop_rate_warning_psi_per_min` (converted to per-second)
- Cross-correlation: pressure drop >2.0 PSI/min AND temp rise >1.0 F/min
- Ground idle RPM threshold: 800 RPM
- Trend window: 30 seconds

## Required SimVars

- `GeneralEngOilPressure` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngOilTemperature` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngCombustion` (per engine)
- `GeneralEngRpm` (per engine)

## Evaluation Interval

5 seconds

## Limitations

- Some MSFS aircraft do not model oil pressure accurately at all RPM ranges
- SimConnect oil temperature may lag behind actual thermal conditions
- Cannot detect oil quantity directly (MSFS does not expose oil quantity as a SimVar)
